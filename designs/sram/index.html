Introduction

Police Legal Sciences has provided forensic analysis and expert testimony for law enforcement agencies across the region for over two decades. Their reputation depends on precision, thoroughness, and the ability to communicate complex technical findings in court.

The problem

With their growth, managing case files, chain of custody documentation, and evidence tracking became increasingly difficult. Their existing system required significant manual data entry and left room for critical errors. They needed a solution that could handle the complexity of their work without oversimplifying the process.

Content mapping

We began by mapping every step of their forensic workflow, from initial evidence receipt through final report delivery. This revealed numerous decision points, regulatory requirements, and quality control checkpoints that couldn't be eliminated or simplified. The system needed to embrace this complexity, not hide from it.

Finding the right amount of complexity

Rather than trying to make the interface "simple," we focused on making it clear and comprehensive. Each workflow step shows exactly what's required, why it matters, and what happens next. Context-sensitive help appears exactly when needed. The interface doesn't pretend the work is simple—it helps users navigate the necessary complexity with confidence.

Example of necessary complexity

The chain of custody tracking includes detailed timestamps, handler information, storage conditions, and access logs. While this creates a dense interface, every field serves a legal and operational purpose. Our design organizes this information hierarchically and provides clear visual relationships between connected data points, making the complexity manageable without reducing it.
